What We Do

Creation: A Celebration of Art, Music, and Life is much more than an arts and culture festival; it’s a movement designed to break down barriers, foster connection across diverse communities, and focus on the well-being of marginalized people. At its heart, Creation aims to desegregate creative scenes that are often siloed, bringing together artists, performers, and audiences from all walks of life. We reject the status quo by curating an inclusive, accessible space that accurately reflects the diversity of our community. To us, diversity is more than a buzzword. It's a commitment to a way of life.

Our Mission

Creation thrives on three major ideas: 1. understanding the experiences of other humans strengthens our collective humanity, 2. the job of the arts is to achieve that goal, and 3. the health of a society is judged by how we treat its most vulnerable citizens. With a focus on accessibility—physical, neurological, and emotional—Creation ensures that every attendee feels welcomed and safe. Spaces for decompression, access to community resources and organizations, and a priority on inclusivity for disabled friends and family are fundamental to our event.

Our Work

Creation Fest is held in parts of town that are underutilized, with no pre-conditions associated with it. We bring together disparate parts of the music community to curate shows that represent the depth of skill & talent that Cleveland has to offer. Previous venues include: The Brownhoist, Blk Punx Press, the Magalen, and Negative Space Studios.

In the past, Creation has partnered with the Julia de Bergos Cultural Center, the LGBT Center of Cleveland, the Tamir Rice Cultural Center, and Mx. Juneteenth to provide resources to the community.

Creation positions Cleveland as a leading city for progressive, inclusive arts and culture, in step with cities like Detroit and Chicago. We intend to go beyond the mainline festival and have smaller events hosted/sponsored by Creation itself. One example is Renaissance Noire, an event taking place at the Bop Stop on February 13th, 2025 - a celebration of musicians who are part of the Black Diaspora, but partake in music of all genres. We are currently partnered with Cleveland Rocks! PPF, and are planning to make Creation 5.0 our biggest celebration yet.

Creation always includes a collaborative art piece made by our attendees, interactive art installations, and a rave as the finale.

Notable alum: Apostle Jones, Mixed Feelings, Onya Nurve (Ru Paul's Drag Race), Afi Scruggs, Joi Grant
